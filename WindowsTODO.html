This site is intended as an intermediate repository for more or less finalized ideas and plans for the R statistical system. Most parts of the site are open to the public, and we welcome discussions on the ideas, but please do not take them for more than that, in particular there is no commitment to actually carry out the plans in finite time unless expressedly stated.

Enhancements

There are currently no plans to make Windows-specific enhancements to R. R-core members feel that the aim of providing equivalent functionality on Windows as on Unix/Linux has been achieved as well as is practicable with the limited expertise available.

There are many opportunities for contributions by Windows programmers, including the lists below.

Bugs

A few things we know about, but do not know how to solve.
  • The enhanced metafiles produced by R do not work well in some applications, including Word. We suspect that in part the problem is in Word's reader.
  • There are a number of aspects of the toolkit (GraphApp) that are not wholly satisfactory to experienced Windows users.

User suggestions

Suggestions include:

  • Connections to external databases, ability to read/write Excel and dBase files, .... [package RODBC can do a lot of this.]
  • Use separate threads for the R engine, graphics and overall control (and thereby avoid polling for Windows events).
  • Adding the ability to profile (function Rprof).
  • The ability to write WMF on Unix. (This means vector WMF, not the bitmaps that epstool and GSView make.)
